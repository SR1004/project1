Welcome to OpenStreetMap

OpenStreetMap (OSM) is a collaborative project to create a free, editable map of the world. It is often referred to as the "Wikipedia of maps" because it is built by volunteers who contribute and maintain geographic data.

üåç What is OpenStreetMap?
OpenStreetMap is an open-source mapping platform that provides geographic data to anyone, for free. Unlike proprietary maps, OSM allows users to view, edit, and use the data for any purpose, as long as they credit the project.

Why is OpenStreetMap Used?

OpenStreetMap is used for a wide range of applications, including:

  • Navigation: Many apps and devices use OSM data for routing and directions.
  • Urban Planning: City planners use OSM to analyze and improve infrastructure.
  • Disaster Response: OSM is often used to map affected areas during natural disasters.
  • Research: Academics and researchers use OSM data for studies on geography, transportation, and more.
  • Custom Maps: Businesses and organizations create custom maps tailored to their needs.
